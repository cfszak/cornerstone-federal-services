How We Work

We're a small business built around one habit: documenting a part before we offer it, not after someone asks. Here's what that means in practice.

Country of origin, checked per item

We confirm manufacturing origin and, where required, substantial transformation for the specific SKU we're quoting — not assumed from a brand name or a country label. This is a standard we hold ourselves to on every quote, and we're upfront when we haven't yet completed that check on a given item.

TAA compliance, treated as a design constraint

For any item bound for a federal solicitation with a Trade Agreements Act requirement, we source with that constraint in mind from the start — rather than trying to retrofit compliance onto whatever's cheapest. Where we can't yet confirm compliance on a specific part, we say so rather than guess.

What this looks like today

Cornerstone Federal Services is a new small business. We're building our documentation process and past performance record starting with straightforward, well-specified items — fasteners and industrial hardware — precisely so we can prove this approach out on a small scale before applying it to larger, more complex categories like electrical and switchgear components.
